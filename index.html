Where would I actually put it?

So… where would I actually put it?

Here are the most common ways everyday people invest:

📈

Index Funds

Set it and mostly forget it.

An index fund is like buying a tiny slice of hundreds of companies at once. Low fees, low effort, historically strong returns.

How to access: Available through Fidelity, Vanguard, or Schwab. Search for “S&P 500 index fund.”

🧺

ETFs

Like index funds, but you can buy them like a stock.

ETFs are baskets of investments that trade on the stock market. Popular examples include VTI or VOO.

How to access: Available on most brokerage apps, including Fidelity, Schwab, and Robinhood.

🏦

High-Yield Savings Account

Better than letting it sit in checking.

If you’re not ready to invest yet, a high-yield savings account can be a simple place to park cash while earning interest.

How to access: Try banks like Ally, Marcus, or SoFi.
